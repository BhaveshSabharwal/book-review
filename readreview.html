Book reviews:

Mystery/Thriller:

Gone Girl

Gillian Flynn
4.4 Stars




Review by Sarah Miller, February 15, 2021
An addictive thriller with unexpected twists and complex characters. Flynn's exploration of marriage and deception keeps you guessing until the very end.

The Girl with the Dragon Tattoo

Stieg Larsson
4.9 Stars




Review by James Anderson, July 28, 2022
A gripping mystery with a brilliant hacker protagonist, Lisbeth Salander. Larsson's intricate plot and suspenseful storytelling make this a must-read for thriller fans.

Big Little Lies

Liane Moriarty
3.6 Stars




Review by Emily Clark, October 5, 2023
A compelling blend of mystery and domestic drama. Moriarty skillfully weaves together secrets and lies among a group of mothers, creating a suspenseful and engaging narrative.


Science Fiction:

Dune

Frank Herbert
4.6 Stars




Review by Frank Herbert, March 10, 2020
An epic tale of politics, religion, and ecology in a distant future. Herbert's world-building and complex characters make "Dune" a masterpiece of science fiction literature.

Ender's Game

Orson Scott Card
4.1 Stars




Review by Frank Herbert,November 19,2021
A thought-provoking novel about a young boy's journey through military training to defend Earth from an alien threat. Card's exploration of leadership and morality is captivating.

Neuromancer

William Gibson
4.7 Stars




Review by Daniel White, April 3, 2024
A groundbreaking cyberpunk novel that introduced the concept of the Matrix. Gibson's gritty prose and visionary depiction of a future dominated by technology are ahead of its time.


Fiction:

To Kill a Mockingbird

Harper Lee
3.8 Stars




Review by Alice Johnson, September 8, 2020
A timeless classic that explores racism and injustice through the eyes of Scout Finch in the American South. Lee's vivid characters and powerful storytelling make it an essential read.

The Great Gatsby

F. Scott Fitzgerald
4.3 Stars




Review by Michael Brown, December 22, 2022
A dazzling portrayal of the Jazz Age and the American Dream's dark side. Fitzgerald's prose is captivating, and the tragic tale of Jay Gatsby is both glamorous and poignant.

1984

George Orwell
4.5 Stars




Review by Emma Wilson, June 14, 2023
A chilling dystopian novel that remains disturbingly relevant. Orwell's depiction of a totalitarian society and its impact on individual freedom is a stark warning for modern times.


Historical Fiction:


The Nightingale

Kristin Hannah
3.9 Stars




Review by Laura Harris, January 7, 2024
A poignant story of two sisters in Nazi-occupied France, showcasing their resilience and courage. Hannah's evocative writing brings history to life with emotional depth.




All the Light We Cannot See

Anthony Doerr
4.8 Stars




Review by Alice, August 30, 2021
A beautifully crafted novel set during World War II, following the lives of a blind French girl and a German soldier. Doerr's prose is lyrical, painting a vivid portrait of wartime Europe.

The Book Thief

Markus Zusak
4.2 Stars




Review by Alice, May 1, 2022
A haunting narrative set in Nazi Germany, told from the perspective of Death. Zusak's exploration of humanity, loss, and the power of storytelling is both heart-wrenching and profound.
